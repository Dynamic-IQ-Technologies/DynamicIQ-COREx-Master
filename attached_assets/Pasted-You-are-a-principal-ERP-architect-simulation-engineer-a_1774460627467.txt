You are a principal ERP architect, simulation engineer, and decision-intelligence designer.
Your task is to extend an existing ERP/MRO system with a Digital Twin Engine that mirrors real-world operations and allows users to simulate future operational scenarios before they happen.

OBJECTIVE

Transform the platform from a static planning system into a forward-looking decision simulator that enables executives, planners, and operations leaders to understand risk, cost, and downtime impact before action is required.

The system must:

Create live digital twins of assets, inventory, and maintenance schedules
Simulate disruptions and demand changes
Quantify operational, financial, and risk impact
Present outcomes in executive-friendly language
🧠 CORE CAPABILITIES
1️⃣ Digital Twin Engine (Foundational)

Create a Digital Twin Engine that continuously mirrors:

Assets
Health, usage, failure probabilities
Inventory
On-hand, in-transit, safety stock, buffers
Maintenance Schedules
Preventive, corrective, predictive tasks

Twins must remain synchronized with real system data and update in near-real time.

2️⃣ Twin Modeling Requirements

Each digital twin must include:

State variables (health, quantity, timing)
Dependencies (asset ↔ parts ↔ suppliers)
Constraints (lead times, compliance, capacity)
Cost parameters (downtime, holding, expedite)

Models must be versioned, timestamped, and auditable.

3️⃣ Scenario Simulation Module

Enable users to run “What-If” simulations, including but not limited to:

Supplier failure or shutdown
Lead-time increase or doubling
Sudden demand spikes
Asset failure acceleration
Maintenance deferral

Each scenario must be configurable and repeatable.

4️⃣ Impact Analysis Output

For every simulation, calculate and display:

Expected downtime (hours / days)
Revenue or production impact ($)
Inventory shortages or excess
Maintenance backlog impact
Compliance risk exposure
Confidence level of results

Example Executive Output:

“If Supplier B fails within 30 days, expected downtime increases by 42 hours with a projected cost impact of $680K.”

5️⃣ Recommended Mitigation Actions

After simulation, automatically generate ranked mitigation strategies:

Alternate sourcing
Inventory buffer adjustments
Maintenance schedule acceleration
Expedite vs defer analysis

Each recommendation must include:

Risk reduction (%)
Cost impact ($)
Time saved or lost
Trade-off explanation
6️⃣ Executive Visualization Layer

Provide a Digital Twin Simulation View:

Side-by-side: Current State vs Simulated State
Clear KPIs (Downtime, Cost, Risk)
Timeline impact visualization
Plain-language summary

Executives should immediately understand:

“Here’s the operational impact before it happens.”

7️⃣ System Integration Points (Mandatory)

Embed Digital Twin outputs into:

Material Requirements Report
Asset Intelligence tab
Supply Chain Risk Radar
Executive dashboards
Planning & forecasting workflows

Simulation results must be contextual and actionable.

🔌 TECHNICAL CONSTRAINTS
Do not alter existing transactional logic
Implement as modular simulation services
Support async scenario execution
Allow scenario cloning and comparison
Feature-toggle by tenant or role
📊 DATA MODELS TO ADD
Digital Twin (Asset / Inventory / Schedule)
Twin State Snapshot
Scenario Definition
Simulation Run
Impact Assessment
Mitigation Recommendation
Simulation Audit Log

All records must support time-series analysis.

🧪 ACCEPTANCE CRITERIA

✔ Digital twins stay synchronized with live data
✔ Users can run and compare multiple scenarios
✔ Impacts are quantified and explainable
✔ Mitigation actions are generated automatically
✔ Executives can understand results without training

🏁 SUCCESS DEFINITION

A leader can ask:

What happens if this supplier fails?
What if lead times double?
What if demand spikes next quarter?

…and receive clear, quantified answers before damage occurs.
