Update the app’s Bill of Materials (BOM) page to meet full industry-standard functionality used in aviation, manufacturing, and MRO systems. The structure should support multi-level BOMs, find number designations, and subcomponent categorization.

Functional Requirements:

BOM Hierarchy

Enable multi-level (parent-child) structure for assemblies, sub-assemblies, and components.

Include automatic numbering or hierarchy indicators (e.g., 1.0, 1.1, 1.1.1).

Allow expanding/collapsing of subcomponents for better visualization.

Find Number Designation

Add a Find Number field for each line item to uniquely identify part position within an assembly.

Automatically generate find numbers sequentially or allow manual override.

Support referencing find numbers in linked documents (drawings, work orders, engineering change forms).

Subcomponent Categorization

Introduce Category fields (e.g., Electrical, Mechanical, Hardware, Consumable, Subassembly).

Enable filtering and sorting of BOM items by category.

Allow roll-up summaries by category (e.g., total weight, cost, or quantity per category).

Part and Revision Control

Integrate Part Number, Description, UOM, Quantity, Unit Cost, and Extended Cost fields.

Include Revision, Effectivity Date, and Status (Active/Obsolete) fields to track configuration control.

Allow linking to the Part Master record for real-time updates.

Document and Drawing Links

Add an attachment or hyperlink section for related Drawings, Specs, and Manuals.

Allow direct access to documents from the BOM line.

Change Management

Enable tracking of BOM Revisions with version history and effective date ranges.

Include optional integration with Engineering Change Orders (ECOs) for traceability.

Usability Enhancements

Add a “Clone” or “Copy from Template” function to quickly create new BOMs based on existing structures.

Provide mass update capability for cost, revision, or quantity changes.

Support export/import to Excel or CSV for bulk edits.

Validation & Controls

Prevent duplicate part numbers within the same assembly.

Automatically calculate total cost and component count.

Include visual indicators for missing required fields or inactive parts.

UI/UX Requirements:

Use a tree-view or collapsible grid layout for easy navigation through BOM levels.

Add color-coded icons for assemblies vs. components.

Include filters for “Active Only,” “Show Subcomponents,” and “By Category.”

Make the interface responsive for tablet and desktop use.

Automation / Integration:

Auto-pull part details (description, cost, category, vendor) from the Part Master database.

Integrate with Work Orders to generate required material lists automatically.

Option to sync with Inventory for availability and stock status.
