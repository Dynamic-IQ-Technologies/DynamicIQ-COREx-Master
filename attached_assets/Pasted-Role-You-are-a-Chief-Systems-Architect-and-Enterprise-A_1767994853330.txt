Role:
You are a Chief Systems Architect and Enterprise Analytics Engineer working within an existing production business management platform.

Objective:
Design and implement a new executive page titled:

Organizational Scorecard

This page must provide a single, authoritative executive view of financial performance, operational execution, and inventory readiness, enabling leadership to quickly assess organizational health and risks.

1️⃣ PAGE PURPOSE & DESIGN PRINCIPLES

Executive-level visibility (CEO / CFO / COO)

Snapshot-based, defensible metrics

Minimal cognitive load

Trend-aware, action-oriented

Read-only, decision-grade data

The page must not query raw transactional tables directly.
All values must be derived from pre-aggregated analytical views or snapshot services.

2️⃣ PAGE STRUCTURE (TOP-DOWN EXECUTIVE FLOW)
Organizational Scorecard
├─ Financial Performance
├─ Operations Performance
├─ Inventory Status
└─ Insights & Alerts

3️⃣ FINANCIAL PERFORMANCE SECTION
Metrics to Display

Weekly Sales Revenue

Current week total

Prior week comparison

% change (trend indicator)

Year-to-Date Sales & Profit

YTD revenue

YTD gross profit

Profit margin %

Current Bank Balance

Real-time or last reconciled value

Last updated timestamp

Accounts Receivable Aging

Total AR balance

% of receivables over 30 days

Visual aging breakdown (0–30 / 31–60 / 60+)

Accounts Payable Aging

Total AP balance

% of payables over 30 days

Aging distribution

Presentation

KPI cards with trend arrows

Aging donut or stacked bar visualization

Red / amber / green risk indicators

4️⃣ OPERATIONS SECTION
6. Open Sales Orders

Display a table of open sales orders, including for each order:

Sales Order ID

Customer

Budgeted expense / work value

Actual cost incurred to date (if available)

Percentage of work completed

Estimated completion date

Status indicator

7. Dated Sales Orders

Defined as:

Signed sales orders with a future delivery date

For this subset display:

Order ID

Customer

Order value

Scheduled delivery date

Days until delivery

Revenue not yet recognized

5️⃣ INVENTORY SECTION
8. Ready-to-Sell Inventory

Total quantity of ready-to-sell parts

Total inventory value

Breakdown by product / part category (optional drill-down)

9. Inventory Awaiting Work or Certification

Quantity of parts awaiting:

Repair

Certification

Quality approval

Estimated value tied up

Aging since receipt

Use status-based classification, not free-text fields.

6️⃣ INSIGHTS & ALERTS (EXECUTIVE VALUE)

Auto-generated insights such as:

“AR over 30 days exceeds X% — potential cash flow risk”

“Open orders are X% complete but Y% of budget consumed”

“Inventory awaiting certification increased week-over-week”

“Upcoming dated orders represent $X in future revenue”

These must be derived, not manually entered.

7️⃣ DATA MODEL REQUIREMENTS

Minimum required analytical fields:

Order value

Budgeted cost

Actual cost (optional)

Completion percentage

Inventory quantity & status

Unit cost & extended value

AR/AP balances with aging buckets

Snapshot date

All calculations must be centralized in a metrics service, not the UI.

8️⃣ PERFORMANCE & GOVERNANCE

Snapshot refresh: daily (default)

Role-based access (executive only)

Read-only UI

Export to PDF / Excel

Audit log for page access

Target load time: < 300ms

9️⃣ BUSINESS OUTCOMES (MUST BE ACHIEVED)

This scorecard must directly enable:

Improved visibility into weekly performance trends

Early identification of cash flow risks

Early detection of operational bottlenecks

Clear tracking of progress on open sales orders

Faster, data-driven executive decisions

10️⃣ SUCCESS TEST (EXECUTIVE LENS)

An executive should be able to answer in under 60 seconds:

“Are we financially healthy this week?”

“Do we have a cash flow problem forming?”

“Are operations ahead or behind plan?”

“Where is inventory stuck?”

“What needs attention now?”
